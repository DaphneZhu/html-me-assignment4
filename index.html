Top 3 Memberships Worth The Money For Parents with Young Kids In St. Louis Area

#1 St. Louis Science Center Membership

#2 The Magic House Membership

#3 Membership for Missouri Botanical Garden, Butterfly House and Shaw Nature Reserve

# 1 St. Louis Science Center Membership

We all know St. Louis Science Center is free to get in and why should someone get the Membership? And why I put it on my #1 money worth list? It's worth the time read thorough my reasons. First, let's do some simply calculations here. Numbers are my best friends when it comes to value comparisions. If we have a family of four, two parents and two kids, one is 2 and one is 5. The family plan to spend their Saturday in the Science Center because it's a cold winter day. Here is their plan: 10-11 am: discovery-room $20.00 11-12 am: a cute educational panda show at omnimax-theater $39.80 1-2 pm: free explore paking (the free parking lot is full becuase the weather) $10.00 total $68.80 Let's take a look at Membership price first. For $89 per year, you can get Family & friends level, which includes FREE PARKING for one car($10/car without membership), 6 FREE Planetarium Show tickets ($6/ticket without membership), 6 FREE Discovery Room tickets ($5/ticket without membership), 6 Discounted special exhibition tickets for every visit. It also comes with 10 Free OMNIMAX Theater tickets for this level of membership, which will cost $7.95-10.95/adult and $6.95-8.95/child depend on the show. $69.80 for one day vs. $89 all year long free parking and free discovery-room tickets. Parents who have kids enjoy science and learning would find the membership is so worth the money. Plus, if the family ever travel to other cities, St. Louis Science Center is affilicated with most other science museums in the country, some are even international affilications. You can get in for FREE if you are a member for most of them. Check the ASTC website for more details when making your travel plans. It can easily save hundrends of dollars if you are planning to visit local science realted museums.

#2 The Magic House Membership

The Magic Houseis a magical place for kids. It's my little one's favorite place to go since she was 1. The place is built base on hands-on concepts. It encourages kids to explore their 5 senses, opening their minds toward science and technology, literature and art, exploring their physical ability safely. It's the first I would recommand to moms who have young kids. The only reason it's on the number 2 list is the price, $150 for family membership with two parents and all the kis who live in the same hosuehold. $150/year is not very attractive number. However, the Magic House has a special 50% sale for it's membership twice/year. One is in August, and one is in November. Plus, it's affilicated with 200 ACM network which can save 50% on the tickets. Compare to the daily admission $12/person (age one and up), for a family of four, two parents and two kids, one is 2 and one is 5. $12 x 4 = $48 / visit vs $150/ year with unlimited visits, not mention if they get it on the sales day. More kids one family have, more money they can save to be a member. It's a great Christmas gift idea or birthday gift for in-laws who have young kids.

#3 Membership for Missouri Botanical Garden, Butterfly House and Shaw Nature Reserve

Missouri Botanical Garden is one of the places where you can get the most amazing scenary pictures in St. Louis. Being the second largest botancial garden in North America, it's a home of many plants. If you enjoy nature like I do, it's definitely a must membership to have in your wallet. How about kids then? The Children's Garden is an outdoor advanture for kids to explore the beauty of the nature. The down side is that the children's Garden opens from April - October each year. It closes in other months due to the weather. The great thing about the membership for Missouri Botanical Garden is that one membership for three accesses. $75 for a family membership, it gives the accesses to Butterfly House and Shaw Nature Reserve. Butterfly House sits next to Faust Park and St. Louis Carousel. It offers many kids educational programs all year long. Although the facility is small, with the park next by and St. Louis Carousel in a walking distance, kids will have a great fun day. Now let's do some calculations why the membership is worth the money. For a family of four, two parents and two kids, one is 2 and one is 5. Botanical Graden offer free admission to St. Louis County residents on Wednesdays and Saturdays before noon. We assume that you are a St. Louis County residents, you would like to go there on Saturday Morning, it only left Butterfly House and Shaw Nature Reserve. Butterfly House $8x 2 adults= $16 $5x2 kids = $10 $16+$10 = $26 Shaw Nature Reserve $5 x 2 adults = $10 total $36 $36 for visit the places once a year. With $75, you can get unlimited accesses to all three places all year long.

Being a member of public museums and parks is not only being a beneficiary of discounts, but also a way to support our community. The museums and parks are the results of collections of human effort and wisdom and we can be part of it.
